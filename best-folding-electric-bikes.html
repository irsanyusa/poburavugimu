Best Folding Electric Bikes to Make Travel a Breeze

Publish date: 2024-09-05

As you no doubt know from looking at some of the best electric bikes out there, e-bikes are a fantastic option for those who want to get around without a car, but don’t necessarily want to pedal the whole way. But they can also be unwieldy and inconvenient (and what is an electric bike supposed to be, if not convenient?), which is why you may want to opt for a folding version, instead. We’ve rounded up the best folding electric bikes, so hop on and let’s go for a spin.

What to consider when choosing folding electric bikes

According to Joan Denizot, cycling expert at Zize Bikes, you should consider the following when picking out a folding electric bike:

Price

First, determine your budget range and what you’re comfortable spending. While you might not want to spend too much, “Higher-priced models often come with advanced features and better build quality,” says Denizot.

Make/brand

Always do your research and look for reputable brands, known for their quality and customer support. Check the customer reviews and feedback, too.

Design

“Assess the bike's folding mechanism, ease of use, and how compact it becomes when folded,” suggests Denizot. “Choose a design that suits your storage and transportation needs.”

Intended use

Whether you’re planning on commuting, going for recreational rides, cycling off-road, or hauling cargo will all inform your decision.

Size

“Ensure the bike's folded size fits your storage space, whether it's in a closet, car trunk, or under your desk,” says Denizot.

Range and battery

Be sure to check the bike's battery capacity and estimated range so you know you can comfortably get to where you plan on going.

Motor type

“Consider hub motors for flat terrain and mid-drive motors for hilly terrain and better power distribution,” advises Denizot.

Pedal assist levels

If you want to be able to ride exactly the way you like, “Look for multiple pedal assist levels to customize your riding experience,” says Denizot.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Is a folding eBike worth it?

“A folding eBike is a valuable investment, especially when space is limited,” says Haamer. “These bikes offer the convenience of compact storage and the assistance of an electric motor, making even uphill commutes a breeze. They’re perfect for urban dwellers and commuters seeking an efficient and space-saving mode of transportation, allowing for easy storage in small living spaces and hassle-free multimodal commuting. Make sure you're also looking at used and refurbished options to save some cash!”

What are the disadvantages of a folding bike?

Folding bikes tend to be heavier than non-folding bikes, due to the extra parts required for folding and unfolding, but the disadvantages don’t end there, unfortunately. “The biggest disadvantage of a folding bike is that they have a lower payload capacity, due to the fact that the frame folds up,” warns Swartz. “Also, over time the clamps on lower-quality folding e-bikes can bend, making it difficult to fold or unfold the e-bike. Another disadvantage is that they have smaller wheels and wheelbases, which means if you’re over 6’2” the riding position might feel a bit awkward.” You may also need a specialized bike lock.

What size folding bike is best?

“The ideal size for a folding bike depends on your individual needs and how you plan to use it,” says Haamer. “Your choice should consider factors such as your height, storage space, and intended use. Folding bikes also come in various wheel sizes, including 16-inch, 20-inch, and 26-inch wheels, and each size has its advantages.”

16-inch Wheels

“These are the smallest and most compact, making them an excellent choice for easy storage and portability,” says Haamer. “They’re suitable for short commutes and urban travel, where convenience and compactness are top priorities. However, they may not provide the smoothest ride, especially on rough terrain.”

20-inch Wheels

Haamer explains that these strike the right balance between compactness and ride comfort, adding that, “They’re versatile and suitable for various terrains, including urban streets and some light trails. 20-inch folding bikes are often a popular choice for commuters and travelers.”

26-inch Wheels

Admittedly less common in folding bikes, these larger wheels will give you an experience closer to that of a full-sized bike. “They’re ideal for riders who prioritize ride quality and plan to use their folding bike for longer distances or on more challenging terrain,” says Haamer.

Can you ride long-distance on a folding bike?

“You can ride long distances on a folding e-bike, but the range will depend on the battery capacity and your pedaling effort,” says Denizot. “High-capacity batteries can provide longer ranges, making them suitable for extended rides. However, it's important to plan your route with access to charging points if you're embarking on exceptionally long journeys. Additionally, consider the comfort of the bike for longer rides and invest in ergonomic accessories, like a comfortable saddle and padded grips.”

Regarding the comfort level, Haamer also suggests that a bike with larger wheels is going to make for a smoother long-distance ride.

Is it harder to pedal a folding bike?

“It’s not harder to pedal a folding e-bike, but you have to get used to the geometry difference,” says Connor Swegle, CMO and co-founder at Priority Bicycles. “You’re sitting more forward because of the smaller frame size, particularly if the wheels are small. If you’re not used to a folding bike, give yourself some time to adapt and react on the road as you start riding.”

Are folding bikes as good as normal bikes?

“Folding bikes are as good as normal bikes in many aspects, but they have some trade-offs,” says Denizot. “They excel in terms of portability and storage convenience, making them ideal for urban commuting, public transport, or travel. However, they may have smaller wheels and frames, which can affect ride comfort and speed. The choice between a folding bike and a regular bike depends on your specific needs and priorities. Folding bikes are excellent for certain scenarios, while regular bikes are better for others, such as high-speed cycling or off-road adventures.” Take that into consideration when you’re looking for the best electric mountain bikes, and other e-bikes for recreational treks.
