Why cats love cardboard boxes

Publish date: 2024-07-09

Despite having a soft bed or couch at their disposal, cats prefer an empty and uncomfortable-looking cardboard box.

Although cardboard boxes are mainly used for storing or transporting various things and we do not pay much attention to them, they are an object of desire for cats.

More than one cat owner has noticed that their pet, despite having a soft bed or couch at their disposal, prefers an empty and uncomfortable-looking cardboard box.

Why are cats interested in it? It turns out that this need can be explained.

ADVERTISEMENT

Why do cats love cardboard boxes?

Cats are happy to squeeze into cardboard boxes, even if they seem small and uncomfortable at first glance. This is because the limited space inside them gives them a sense of security.

When the need arises, a small area allows them to shut themselves off from the world and calm down the emotions that are starting to build up. A dark place is also a good material for a hideout and a safe place allows them to observe what is happening around them.

In addition, heat accumulates in a small box because cardboard insulates quite well, which may be another explanation for why cats often abandon a soft, comfortable sleeping place in favour of a cramped box.

The matter was investigated by experts

ADVERTISEMENT

The value of cat boxes has been confirmed by research conducted by a team of scientists led by Claudia Vinke, a professor at Utrecht University in the Netherlands. Researchers at an animal shelter selected 19 cats and divided them into two groups. Ten cats were given a box in their box, while the rest were not. What was the result of this study?

The animals that had the cardboard box in their kennel were much more relaxed than those that had to do without it. Their stress levels dropped much faster.
